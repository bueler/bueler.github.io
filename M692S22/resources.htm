Math 692 Graduate Seminar (Spring 2002)

Mathematics of Machine Learning

Resource List

One goal of the seminar will be to identify and collect some high-quality open resources. The list below are some possibilities.

Please send any additions you want to elbueler@alaska.edu and I will add them to the list. They need to be open resources, so make sure to send a link.
  • Bottou, L., Curtis, F. E., & Nocedal, J. (2018). Optimization methods for large-scale machine learning. SIAM Review, 60(2), 223--311.
    • Additional slides.
  • Bronstein, M. M., Bruna, J., LeCun, Y., Szlam A., and Vandergheynst, P., (2017). Geometric deep learning: Going beyond Euclidean data, IEEE Signal Processing Magazine 34(4), 18--42.
  • Deisenroth, M. P., Faisal, A. A., and Ong C. S., Mathematics for Machine Learning, Cambridge University Press 2020.
  • Higham, C. F., & Higham, D. J. (2019). Deep learning: An introduction for applied mathematicians. SIAM Review, 61(4), 860--891.
  • LeCun, Y., Bengio, Y. & Hinton, G. (2015). Deep learning. Nature 521, 436--444.
  • Sun, S., Cao, Z., Zhu, H., & Zhao, J. (2019). A survey of optimization methods from a machine learning perspective. IEEE Transactions on Cybernetics, 50(8), 3668--3681.
  • Hamilton, W., Graph Representation Learning, Synthesis Lectures on Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning, Vol. 14, No. 3, Morgan & Claypool Publishers 2020.
Also, there are fantastic interactive machine learning articles at Distill, including:
  • Goh, (2017). Why momentum really works, Distill.
  • Sanchez-Lengeling, et al., (2021). A gentle introduction to graph neural networks, Distill.
