Required text:   Griva, Nash, and Sofer, Linear and Nonlinear Optimization, 2nd ed., SIAM Press 2009, ISBN-13: 978-0-898716-61-0


Links:
  • Another good textbook: Nocedal & Wright, Numerical Optimization, 2nd ed., Springer 2006
  • Free, online, introductory book by Cleve Moler on using Matlab for numerical computations.
  • The Wikipedia page on mathematical optimization shows how large is this field of applied mathematics.
  • The principle of least action, the soap bubble (minimal surface) problem, and the beam equation are all calculus of variations problems which date to the 1700s and to Euler and Bernoullis. Optimization is among the oldest mathematical threads!
  • For obscure historical reasons, optimization problems where all functions are linear are called linear programming problems. If the number of variables is large then these problems are harder than you might think.
  • The famous traveling salesperson problem is a combinatorial optimization problem. We will not cover them in the course.
  • Machine learning, and specifically the implementation of artificial neural networks, is nearly a subfield of optimization. A recommended 42 minute introduction is the first video in this MIT course. It shows that even the weak tool of steepest descent is effective at big, important scale.
