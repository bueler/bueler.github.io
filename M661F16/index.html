Links:
  • Free, online, introductory book by Cleve Moler on using Matlab in an introductory numerical course.
  • The Wikipedia page on mathematical optimization shows how large (and confusing) this is as a field of applied mathematics.
  • The principle of least action, the soap bubble (minimal surface) problem, and the beam equation all date to the 1700s and to Euler and Bernoullis. Optimization is among the oldest mathematical threads!
  • The famous traveling salesperson problem is one of the combinatorial optimization problems which we do not cover in the class.
