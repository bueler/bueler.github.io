  • Alternate texts I could have chosen for this course:
    • Chae, Lebesgue Integration. Sticks to the one dimensional case, and may be a good second reading.
    • Folland, Real Analysis: Modern Techniques and Their Applications. Modern and perhaps the best for e.g. research in PDEs or Fourier analysis.
    • Rudin, Real & Complex Analysis. Goes right to the most general case. Focuses on the function-space parts of complex analysis.

  • Wikipedia pages:
    • list of real analysis topics
    • real analysis
    • metric
    • normed vector space
    • Banach space
    • Banach fixed-point theorem, a.k.a. the contractive mapping principle
    • Picard-Lindelof existence theorem for ODEs is an application of the Banach fixed-point theorem
    • iterated function systems (IFS) are collections of contractive maps on a complete metric space; see also the collage theorem
    • discontinuous linear maps
    • Weierstrauss approximation theorem
    • Stone-Weierstrass theorem
    • continuous but nowhere-differentiable function
    • Lebesgue measure
    • the Cantor set and fat Cantor sets
    • Lebesgue integration
    • Lp space
    • measure-theoretic probability theory: The best single argument for the Lebesgue theory is the unification of probability and analysis.
