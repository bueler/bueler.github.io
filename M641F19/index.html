  • Alternate texts I could have chosen for this course:
    • Chae, Lebesgue Integration. Sticks to the one dimensional case, and may be a good second reading.
    • Folland, Real Analysis: Modern Techniques and Their Applications. Modern and perhaps the best for e.g. research in PDEs or Fourier analysis.
    • Rudin, Real & Complex Analysis. Goes right to the most general case. Focuses on the function-space parts of complex analysis.
    • Taylor, An Introduction to Measure and Probability. The best single argument for the Lebesgue theory is the unification of probability and analysis.
